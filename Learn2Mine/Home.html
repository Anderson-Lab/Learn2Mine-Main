Welcome to Learn2Mine!

Learn2Mine hopes to be an effective way for people of all backgrounds and domains specialties to be able learn and practice the growing discipline of data science. You can select from our wide range of lessons by going viewing your profile. Learn2Mine is a cloud-based environment developed to support the teaching of data science. It is an interactive, integrative learning environment developed to introduce students to the constituent data science areas of data mining, computer programming, and statistics. The development of Learn2Mine was influenced by research results in the effectiveness of gamification of educational tools. The primary goal of Learn2Mine is to increase the accessibility of data science and provide a more effective learning environment to a diverse group of students and practitioners by integrating feedback throughout a lesson and engaging the students via gameful (having a games mindset or attitude) experience. Learn2Mine increases the accessibility of data science and analytics by minimizing the need for local computing resources and reducing programming prerequisites by providing lessons that aid in teaching programming and data science concurrently.

Learn2Mine is an open source project developed by Paul Anderson, Clayton Turner, Jack Dierksheide, Thomas Nash, and Renée McCauley at the College of Charleston. To contribute or run your own version of this software, visit https://github.com/Anderson-Lab/Learn2Mine-Main.

To cite Learn2Mine, please use these two references:

Anderson, P.E., Turner, C., Dierksheide, J., and McCauley, R., "An Extensible Online Environment for Teaching Data Science Concepts through Gamification." Proceedings of the 2014 Frontiers in Education Conference, in press, 2014.

Turner, C., Dierksheide, J., and Anderson, P., “Learn2Mine: Data Science Practice and Education through Gameful Experiences.” International Journal of E-Education, E-Business, E-Management and E-Learning, 4(3), 243–248. 2014, doi:10.7763/IJEEEE.2014.V4.338
